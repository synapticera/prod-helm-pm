## Part 1: McKinsey & Company

### McKinsey: State of AI 2025

**Publication:** November 2025
**Sample Size:** Global survey of enterprises
**Focus:** AI adoption, agent deployment, business impact

**Key Findings:**

- 88% of organizations report regular AI use (up 10pp from 2024)
- Only 39% seeing EBIT-level impact (vs 88% deployment = 49pp gap)
- 23% scaling agentic AI systems
- 39% experimenting with AI agents
- High performers 3x more likely to scale agents
- Workflow redesign has biggest effect on EBIT impact

**Key Quote:**

> "The redesign of workflows has the biggest effect on an organization's ability to see EBIT impact from its use of gen AI."

**Helm Relevance:** Validates workflow redesign as critical success factor; supports Design module positioning

**Links:**

- [McKinsey State of AI](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai)

### MIT Media Lab: "Your Brain on ChatGPT" (Cognitive Decline Study)

**Publication:** 2025
**Methodology:** EEG brain activity measurement, 3 participant groups
**Focus:** AI's impact on cognitive function

**Key Findings:**

| Metric                   | Finding                                              |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Memory retention loss    | 83% couldn't recall key points in AI-assisted essays |
| Writing speed increase   | 60% faster with AI                                   |
| Cognitive load reduction | 32% lower (problematic)                              |
| Brain connectivity       | Nearly halved (alpha/theta waves)                    |

**Critical Insight:**

> Participants who attempted tasks on their own first before using AI performed better in both scenarios—showing more strategy, structure, and quality.

**Helm Relevance:** Supports "think first, AI second" workflow design; cognitive wellness opportunity

**Links:**

- [MIT Media Lab: Your Brain on ChatGPT](https://www.media.mit.edu/publications/your-brain-on-chatgpt/)

## Part 3: Gartner

### Gartner: Decision Intelligence 2026

**Publication:** 2025-2026
**Source:** Gartner Hype Cycle for AI
**Focus:** Emerging technology trends

**Key Finding:**

> Decision Intelligence is the #1 emerging technology trend for 2026

**Definition:**

> "Decision Intelligence is the engineering discipline of how decisions are made" — combines LLMs (probabilistic reasoning) with knowledge graphs + rules (symbolic reasoning)

1. 40% of enterprise apps deploying agents by end 2026
2. Governance gap as companies realize agents can't self-regulate
3. EU AI Act compliance pressure
4. ROI crisis—need to understand decisions to measure value

## Part 5: Bain & Company

### Bain: Agentic AI Transformation

**Publication:** 2025
**Focus:** Foundation building, governance, organizational readiness

**Key Finding:**

> "Agentic AI isn't just another wave of automation; it's a structural shift in enterprise technology."

**Three Critical Requirements:**

1. **Real-time Governance**: Explainability, observability, adaptive security
2. **Enterprise Readiness**: Data access, interoperability, DevOps evolution
3. **Transformation Architecture**: Domain-specific platforms, human-in-loop

**Four-Level Agentic AI Progression:**

1. LLM-Powered Information Retrieval
2. Single-Task Agentic Workflows (current capital concentration)
3. Cross-System Workflow Orchestration
4. Multi-Agent Collaborative Constellations (aspirational)

**Investment Allocation:**

- Years 0-5: 5-10% of tech spending on agentic foundations
- Years 5-10: Scale toward 50% of tech spending

**Helm Relevance:** Validates governance as foundational; supports domain specialization strategy

**Links:**

- [Building the Foundation for Agentic AI](https://www.bain.com/insights/building-the-foundation-for-agentic-ai-technology-report-2025/)
- [State of the Art of Agentic AI Transformation](https://www.bain.com/insights/state-of-the-art-of-agentic-ai-transformation-technology-report-2025/)

**KB Location:** [x-project/kb/market-research/2026-01-05-Bain-Agentic-AI-Transformation-Summary.md]

### Bain: Five Proven Actions for Success

1. Set ambitious top-down goals (not bottom-up pilots)
2. Assign accountability to General Managers (not IT)
3. Redesign entire workflows (not isolated use cases)
4. Strategically curate data & application environment
5. Build capabilities through make/buy/partner balance

### Gerlich Study (SBS Swiss Business School): Critical Thinking Correlation

**Publication:** 2025
**Focus:** AI usage patterns and cognitive impact

**Key Findings:**

- Negative correlation between frequent AI usage and critical thinking abilities
- Younger individuals show stronger AI dependence and lower critical thinking
- Higher education correlates with better reasoning even among heavy AI users

**Source:** [Phys.org](https://phys.org/news/2025-01-ai-linked-eroding-critical-skills.html)

### Oxford/International: Cognitive Atrophy Research

**Key Finding:**

> Heavy AI reliance can bypass foundational learning that comes from manual work

**Examples:**

- Young lawyers using AI for case summaries miss deep reasoning development
- Radiologists using AI may see diagnostic skills erode
- Pattern shows across professions: over-reliance dulls human judgment
