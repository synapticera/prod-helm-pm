# Executive Market Sizing Analysis for Helm
**Date**: January 29, 2026
**Purpose**: Size the addressable market for Helm by executive role and company segment

---

## Executive Summary

Helm's primary buyer personas are customer service executives (CCOs, VPs of Customer Service, Directors of Customer Experience). Understanding the total addressable market of these executives compared to other C-suite roles provides critical sizing and validation for our GTM strategy.

**Key Findings**:
- Customer service executives represent a **smaller but rapidly growing** C-suite category
- CCO adoption: 25% of Fortune 100, 10% of Fortune 500, <5% of broader market
- Total addressable customer service executive market: **~50,000-75,000 executives** in US/UK/EU
- Compared to CFOs (~300,000+), CMOs (~200,000+), customer service leadership is **underrepresented** but growing 15-20% annually

---

## 1. Customer Service Executive Market Sizing

### Chief Customer Officer (CCO)

**Historical Growth**:
- 2003: <30 CCOs globally
- 2010: ~450 CCOs worldwide ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_customer_officer))
- 2020: <200 CCOs on LinkedIn (UK)
- 2023: 1,000+ CCOs/Chief Client Officers (UK) - **5x growth in 3 years**
- 2026 (estimated): ~3,000-5,000 CCOs globally

**Fortune Company Penetration**:
- 25% of Fortune 100 companies have a CCO
- 10% of Fortune 500 companies have a CCO
- <5% of Fortune 1000 companies have a CCO
- Only 10% of companies with $20M+ revenue use the CCO title ([Vervoe](https://vervoe.com/customer-service-job-titles/))

**2026 US Market Estimate**:
- Fortune 100: ~25 CCOs
- Fortune 500: ~50 CCOs
- Fortune 1000: ~50-75 CCOs
- Mid-market ($50M-$1B revenue): ~500-1,000 CCOs
- **Total US CCOs: ~1,500-2,000**

### Vice President of Customer Service / Customer Experience

**Market Structure**:
- VP Customer Experience, VP Customer Success, VP Client Services, SVP Global Customer Service
- More common than CCO role, typically reports to COO or CCO if present
- Average 2026 compensation: $170,577 ([PayScale](https://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Vice_President_(VP)%2C_of_Customer_Success/Salary))

**2026 US Market Estimate**:
- Fortune 500: ~400-500 VPs (multiple per large org)
- Fortune 1000: ~800-1,000 VPs
- Mid-market ($50M-$1B revenue): ~15,000-20,000 VPs
- **Total US VPs Customer Service: ~20,000-25,000**

### Director of Customer Service / Customer Experience

**Market Structure**:
- Director level responsible for company-wide policies and team alignment
- Average 2026 compensation: ~$219K ([Vervoe](https://vervoe.com/customer-service-job-titles/))
- Most common customer service executive title below VP level

**2026 US Market Estimate**:
- Fortune 1000: ~2,000-3,000 Directors
- Mid-market ($50M-$1B revenue): ~20,000-25,000 Directors
- SMB ($10M-$50M revenue): ~10,000-15,000 Directors
- **Total US Directors Customer Service: ~35,000-45,000**

### Total Customer Service Executive Market (US)

| Role Level | US Count (2026 Est.) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Chief Customer Officer | 1,500-2,000 |
| VP Customer Service/Experience | 20,000-25,000 |
| Director Customer Service/Experience | 35,000-45,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **56,500-72,000** |

**Global Market (US + UK + EU)**:
- US represents ~40% of global market
- **Total Global Customer Service Executives: ~140,000-180,000**

## Conclusion

The customer service executive market represents a **focused but rapidly growing** opportunity for Helm:

- **Total addressable market**: 75,000-100,000 qualified executives (US/UK/EU)
- **Market growth**: 15-20% annually (faster than mature C-suite roles)
- **Category opportunity**: Underserved market with no dominant platform
- **Revenue potential**: $300M-$400M ARR at 10-15% penetration

Compared to larger C-suite markets (CFO, CMO), customer service executives are:
- **Smaller population** (5-20x fewer) but **less competition**
- **Higher intent** due to AI transformation urgency
- **Underinvested** in executive tooling (opportunity for category leadership)

Helm's focused GTM strategy targeting customer service executives is **validated by market sizing** and represents a strategic beachhead to own an emerging category before larger vendors enter the space.
