## The Three Enterprise Software Eras

### Era 1: Systems of Record (1980s-2010s)

- **Companies**: Salesforce ($35B revenue), SAP, Oracle, Workday
- **Value Prop**: Centralized database of customer/financial/HR records
- **How It Works**: Humans manually enter data into rigid forms
- **Moat**: Switching costs, network effects of data accumulation
- **Problem**: Slow, requires human effort, siloed

### Era 2: Systems of Engagement (2010s-2020s)

- **Companies**: Slack ($13B valuation), Teams, Zoom
- **Value Prop**: Frictionless human communication
- **How It Works**: Consumer-grade UX for workplace collaboration
- **Moat**: Usage network effects
- **Problem**: Creates data silos, no intelligence layer

### Era 3: Systems of Intelligence (2015-2025)

- **Companies**: Tableau, Looker, Alteryx, DataRobot
- **Value Prop**: Turn data into insights via dashboards + predictive analytics
- **How It Works**: Mine structured data → generate reports/predictions
- **Moat**: Data aggregation
- **Problem**: Imprisoned by structured data; can't handle multimodal business communication

### Era 4: Systems of Agents (2025-2030s)

- **Companies**: TBD (Regie, Oliv AI, ConverzAI, **Helm**)
- **Value Prop**: Autonomous digital workers that capture, understand, act on business communication
- **How It Works**: Agents synthesize unstructured + structured data → execute decisions autonomously
- **Moat**: Decision context + governance + integration depth
- **Winner**: Who builds the **decision infrastructure layer**

## Helm's Positioning in Era 4: The Decision Infrastructure Layer

### What Agents Need to Succeed

**Current Problem**: Agents are being deployed but:

- Making decisions without understanding policy/precedent
- No audit trail for compliance
- No way to learn from past decisions
- No governance framework

**What Agents Need**:

1. **Decision Context**: What decisions were made before? How were exceptions handled?
2. **Policy Enforcement**: What rules apply to this decision?
3. **Audit Trail**: Proof of human review + decision logic
4. **Continuous Learning**: How to improve next decision?

### Helm as Decision Infrastructure Layer

**Mine Module** = Capture Decision Traces

- Sits in execution path as agents and humans make decisions
- Records: decision type, inputs, logic, exceptions, outcome
- Creates searchable library of organizational decision precedent
- (Foundation Capital's "Context Graph")

**Design Module** = Build Decision Governance

- Formalizes decision policies from patterns observed in Mine
- Codifies exceptions + precedent rules
- Creates decision templates for agent guidance
- Ensures compliance requirements are embedded

**Manage Module** = Enforce & Learn

- Agents execute decisions within Helm's governance framework
- Continuous monitoring of decision outcomes
- Fleet learning: every decision improves next agent
- Compliance audit trail automatically generated

### Why Helm Wins in Era 4

**Structural Position**:

- Sitting between agents and execution (perfect data capture point)
- Not competing with Salesforce/SAP (agents bypass them)
- Not competing with Slack (agents synthesize across channels)
- Building what agents **need** to succeed autonomously

**Defensible Moat** (Foundation Capital's Theory):

- Decision context data compounds in value (network effects)
- Companies with better precedent libraries make better decisions
- Competitors need years to accumulate equivalent data
- **"Velocity is the only moat"** (Activant Capital): Helm ships fastest because integration is the product

**Market Size** (Foundation Capital's TAM):

- Not $35B (Salesforce CRM market)
- Not $200B (enterprise software market)
- **$4.6T** (labor market Helm will automate/augment)
- By capturing decision infrastructure first, Helm can expand across service functions (Finance, HR, Ops)

## Risk & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Incumbents Move Fast

**Salesforce/ServiceNow** recognize the opportunity and build decision infrastructure themselves.

**Mitigation**:

- Foundation Capital thesis: "Integration is the product." Custom implementation depth = defensibility
- Vertical specialization advantage (service operations)
- 12-18 month head start to build network effects before competitors respond

### Risk 2: Open Source AI Changes Economics

If agents become commoditized, decision infrastructure becomes less valuable.

**Mitigation**:

- Agents are commodity; decision context is defensible
- Organizational memory (decision traces) can't be open-sourced (proprietary to each org)
- Network effects in decision library remain valuable

### Risk 3: Category Name Confusion

Market might call it "Systems of Intelligence 2.0" instead of "Decision Infrastructure."

**Mitigation**:

- Helm owns "Executive Agent for Service Leaders" in market language
- Analysts will call it "Decision Intelligence"
- Internal messaging uses "decision infrastructure"
- No confusion for buyers; just different terminology for different audiences
