## Executive Summary

## Core Insight: AI-Native from Day One

**Second and third-time founders are most enthusiastic about architecting companies with AI embedded from inception**, rather than retrofitting AI into existing operations. This approach enables efficiency gains across all business functions.

### Why This Matters
- **Competitive Advantage**: AI-native architecture provides structural advantages vs. bolt-on AI
- **Operational Efficiency**: AI embedded across all functions from start
- **Founder Preference**: Experienced founders choosing this approach signals market validation

## Emerging Opportunities

### 1. GTM Operationalization with AI

**Trend**: AI functioning as partner throughout entire sales cycle

**Applications:**
- Lead generation and qualification
- Meeting preparation and research
- Real-time conversation guidance
- Post-call analysis and follow-up
- Deal forecasting and pipeline management

**Competitive Advantage:**
- Creates moat through proprietary data
- Improves conversion rates at every stage
- Reduces sales cycle length
- Enables scalable GTM motions

### 2. "Non-Consumption" Markets

**Source**: Box CEO Aaron Levie insight

**Definition**: Problems customers weren't previously spending money to solve

**Why Important:**
- New budget creation vs. budget reallocation
- Less competitive
- Higher willingness to pay for novel solutions
- Larger TAM expansion opportunity

**Examples:**
- Process visibility that was previously invisible
- Insights from unstructured data that was ignored
- Automation of tasks that were "just how things work"
- Decision support where decisions were made on gut feel

**Strategic Implications for Helm:**
- Process mining was "non-consumption" - companies didn't buy visibility before
- Agent deployment management is emerging "non-consumption" opportunity
- Executive function augmentation is nascent "non-consumption" market
