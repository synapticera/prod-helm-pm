### The Emerging Category: What's Forming

There are **multiple names converging on the same concept**, which is actually a great sign that a category is crystallizing:

**Primary Terms Being Used:**

1. **Context Graphs** (Foundation Capital / Jaya Gupta)
2. **Decision Intelligence** (Gartner, IDC, Quantexa)
3. **Execution Intelligence Layer** (Ed Sim's term)
4. **Enterprise Context Management** (Mark Palmer's framing)
5. **Systems of Record for Decisions** (vs. traditional systems of record for data)

### Key Voices & Sources

#### Tier 1: Category Definers

**Jaya Gupta & Ashu Garg (Foundation Capital)** - Published December 22, 2025
Their piece "Context Graphs: AI's Trillion-Dollar Opportunity" is becoming a seminal reference. They coined the term **decision traces**.

Key insight: "Agents don't just need rules. They need access to the decision traces that show how rules were applied, when exceptions were made, and what precedents exist."

**Ed Sim (Boldstart Ventures)** - Weekly newsletter "What's Hot in Enterprise IT/VC"
He's synthesizing across the ecosystem and coined "Execution Intelligence Layer"

**Aaron Levie (Box CEO)** - "Jevon's Paradox for Knowledge Work"
His argument: AI won't shrink knowledge work - it will massively expand it by making it cheaper. The bottleneck shifts from "can we afford to do this analysis?" to "how do we decide what to do with all this insight?"
