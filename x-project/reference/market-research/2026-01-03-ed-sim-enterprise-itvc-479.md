# Ed Sim's "What's Hot in Enterprise IT/VC" Issue #479
**Key Themes for Enterprise AI & Agentic Workflows**

Date: January 2026 (approximately)
Source: https://www.whatshotit.vc/p/whats-in-enterprise-itvc-479
Author: Ed Sim (Boldstart Ventures)
Status: Market Trend Analysis

---

## Executive Summary

Ed Sim's latest newsletter emphasizes that 2026 will be transformational for agentic AI expanding beyond coding into enterprise automation and process optimization. However, he identifies critical infrastructure gaps and competitive dynamics that will determine winners and losers in the space.

**Key Insight:** The real value isn't in automating existing workflows—it's in reimagining them with AI context and optimization in mind.

---

## Main Arguments & Themes

### 1. **AI Agents Are Going Beyond Coding Into Enterprise**

**Thesis:** 2026 marks the expansion of agentic AI workflows from software development into:
- Enterprise process automation
- Business operations optimization
- Cross-functional workflow orchestration

**Implication:** Customer service is a prime candidate for agentic workflows, as it already has complex decision-making, escalation rules, and context requirements.

---

### 2. **Context and Execution Intelligence Are Essential Infrastructure**

Direct quote from the article (implied): Success requires:
- Rich scaffolds orchestrating multiple models
- Memory management and entitlements
- **Mapping organizational workflows with context graphs**

**This is the critical line for Helm positioning:**
> The infrastructure that enables agents to work safely in enterprises is context-driven, not just model-driven.

**What this means:**
- An LLM alone can't run your customer service
- You need context about your organization (policies, precedents, constraints)
- You need memory of how decisions were made
- You need execution intelligence that connects intent to action

This is **exactly** what Helm's Mine/Design/Manage modules do.

---

### 3. **Middleware is Extraordinarily Difficult to Build**

**Sim's prediction:**
- Building middleware layers proves incredibly hard
- Incumbent SaaS providers (Salesforce, etc.) will dominate by integrating agent capabilities
- Large LLM companies will dominate by bundling orchestration
- **Implication:** Pure-play infrastructure plays face headwinds

**Strategic note:** This suggests Helm's positioning should emphasize vertical specialization (customer service) rather than horizontal infrastructure.

---

### 4. **Vertical-Specific Wins Are the Path Forward for Startups**

**Sim's recommendation for startups:**
- Win with narrow, vertical-specific use cases
- Focus on: Finance, IT, Security, and (implied) Customer Service
- Offer immediate time-to-value
- Provide turnkey integrations

**Why verticals win:**
1. Deep domain knowledge
2. Specific regulatory/compliance requirements
3. Ready integrations with vertical-specific tools
4. Clear buyer personas and use cases
5. Defensible competitive position

**Application to Helm:**
Customer Service is a perfect vertical for this thesis:
- Specific agent requirements (escalation, customer context, resolution paths)
- Specific compliance needs (PII, regulatory records)
- Existing tool ecosystem (Salesforce Service Cloud, ticketing, workforce management)
- Clear buyer (VPs/Directors of Customer Service)
- High ROI visibility (CSAT, resolution time, cost-per-contact)

---

### 5. **Process Reimagining, Not Just Automation**

**Key Quote (paraphrased from article):**
> "Why automate something that already exists if you can squeeze more efficiency from doing them differently?"

**This is fundamental to Helm's value prop:**
- Don't just automate the current customer service process
- **Reimagine** the process with context intelligence
- Use decision traces to find optimization opportunities
- Design new workflows that leverage AI agents

**Implication for Helm's messaging:**
- Mine module: Understand current process (baseline)
- Design module: **Reimagine the process** with agent-native design
- Manage module: Execute and continuously improve the new process

This positions Helm not as automation tool, but as **process transformation platform**.

---

### 6. **Fortune 500 Efficiency Gains Are Real But Misattributed**

**Sim's observation:**
- Large companies are achieving margin improvements
- But most gains come from **post-ZIRP hiring corrections** (firing people they over-hired)
- Not from actual AI productivity gains yet
- Real AI productivity is still in early stages

**Implication:**
- The truly transformational AI gains are still ahead
- Companies pursuing process reimagination (like Helm enables) will be early movers
- There's a narrow window to establish category leadership before everyone catches up

---

### 7. **Competitive Headwinds Are Intensifying**

**Sim identifies three forces:**
1. **Chinese government-backed VC** entering the market
2. **AI company IPOs** (like Anthropic potentially) providing massive capital
3. **Cutthroat dynamics** as the market consolidates

**Implication:**
- First-mover advantage in verticals becomes critical
- Category definition and thought leadership become defensible
- You need to own "Decision Intelligence in Customer Service" before someone else does

---

### 8. **Meta's Manus Acquisition Signals Valuable Agent Companies Don't Need Research Labs**

**What Sim observes:**
- Meta acquired Manus (a robotics/agent company)
- This signals that valuable agent companies can exist without proprietary AI research
- The moat comes from domain knowledge and execution, not model innovation

**Application to Helm:**
- You don't need to build your own LLM
- Your defensibility comes from:
  - Deep customer service domain knowledge
  - Proprietary decision trace data
  - Context graph network effects
  - Execution intelligence layer design

---

### 9. **Authenticity Becomes a Rare Asset**

**Observation from Sim:**
- As AI-generated content proliferates, authentic human insight becomes scarce
- Original thinking and clear positioning stand out

**Application to Helm:**
- Establishing thought leadership in "Decision Intelligence for Customer Service" becomes a brand asset
- Original research and frameworks differentiate from competitors
- Clear positioning + consistent messaging = rare authenticity in crowded market
